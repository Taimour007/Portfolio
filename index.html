AmphiGlide Biomimetic Robot (Final Year Project)

The AmphiGlide robot is a pioneering biomimetic system designed to navigate seamlessly between complex aquatic and terrestrial environments. Inspired by the efficient locomotion of cuttlefish and stringrays, this project pushes the boundaries of multidisciplinary engineering.

3D Industrial Design CFD & FEA Mechatronics Integration

Physical prototype validating fin locomotion principles in a controlled tank environment.

Design Innovation

AmphiGlide uses custom-designed, undulating fin modules. Inspired by nature, these fins generate continuous, efficient thrust without traditional propellers, minimizing disturbance. CAD models for fin structure were meticulously created, incorporating elastic properties, while waterproof mechatronics modules house advanced control and propulsion. This design ensures stealth and scalability.

Multidisciplinary Engineering

The project involved a full spectrum of engineering challenges:

  • Mechanism Design: Developing reliable, low-friction linkages for efficient undulating fin motion using custom linkages.
  • CFD Simulation: Extensive hydrodynamic simulations to analyze fluid-structure interaction, optimize drag coefficients for undulating fins, and predict effective thrust generation.
  • FEA of Fin Structure: Material characterization and finite element analysis of the flexible fin membrane to validate structural integrity under high-frequency deformation.
  • Mechatronics & Control: Integration of microcontrollers, customized servos, and sensor arrays, implementing closed-loop control to dynamic fin amplitude and frequency modulation.

Key Results

The project has successfully validated the fin locomotion principle and generated consistent thrust in simulations. Initial physical testing has demonstrated the effectiveness of waterproof mechatronics modules and provided crucial data for control optimization. This proof-of-concept lays the foundation for specialized operations in challenging environments.
