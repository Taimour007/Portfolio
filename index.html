Flagship Engineering

AmphiGlide

Biomimetic Amphibious Robot

Final Year Project

The AmphiGlide project represents the pinnacle of my undergraduate research. It is a highly specialized biomimetic amphibious robot utilizing advanced undulating fin propulsion. The core objective was to engineer a locomotion strategy inspired by marine biology, navigating complex fluid-structure interactions without relying on traditional rotational thrusters.

I spearheaded the complete 3D industrial design process from initial concept sketches to detailed parametric modeling using SolidWorks. Extensive Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Finite Element Analysis (FEA) were conducted via ANSYS to optimize hydrodynamic efficiency and ensure structural durability under varying pressure zones. Prototyping involved high-resolution FDM 3D printing with PETG and ABS to create robust housing for the internal mechatronics architecture. Future iterations are planned to revamp the industrial design aesthetics for broader applications.
